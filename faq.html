What does a conveyancer actually do?

A conveyancer handles the legal and administrative side of buying, selling, or transferring property. That includes reviewing contracts, conducting title and property searches, coordinating with banks and the other party's representatives, preparing settlement documents, and making sure everything complies with South Australian property law.

In short: we do the paperwork and compliance so you don't have to worry about it.

Do I actually need a conveyancer? Is it required by law?

It's not legally required in South Australia, but it's strongly recommended. Property transactions involve binding legal contracts, and getting something wrong can be expensive to fix. Most real estate agents, brokers, and banks will recommend you use one.

Think of it this way: you could do your own plumbing too, but there's a reason most people don't.

What's the difference between a conveyancer and a solicitor?

Both can handle property transactions in South Australia. A solicitor is a qualified lawyer who can also do conveyancing as part of a broader legal practice. A conveyancer is a specialist. It's all we do, every day.

For standard residential property transactions, a conveyancer is usually the right fit. If your transaction involves complex legal disputes or litigation, a solicitor might be more appropriate. We're happy to point you in the right direction if that's the case.

How long does settlement take?

In South Australia, settlement typically takes around 30 to 60 days from when contracts are signed, though this depends on what's agreed in the contract. Some transactions settle faster, and some take longer. This is especially true if there's finance involved or conditions that need to be met first.

We'll give you a clear timeline once we've reviewed your contract, and we'll keep you updated if anything changes along the way.

What is a Form 1?

A Form 1 (also called a Vendor's Statement) is a document that sellers in South Australia are legally required to provide to buyers. It discloses key information about the property, including the title details, any encumbrances (such as easements or caveats), council and water rates, and planning information.

The Form 1 gives the buyer a cooling-off period. If it's not prepared correctly or information is missing, the buyer can void the contract. That's why it's worth having a conveyancer prepare it properly. More about our Form 1 service

I'm a first home buyer. Where do I start?

Start by getting your finances sorted with a broker or your bank, so you know what you can afford. Then start looking. Once you've found a place and want to make an offer (or have had one accepted), that's when you get in touch with us.

We'll review the contract before you sign anything, explain what all the terms mean, and handle everything from there through to settlement. If you want to chat before you've even started looking, that's fine too. We're happy to walk you through what the process looks like so you know what to expect.

Are there grants or concessions for first home buyers in SA?

Yes. South Australia offers a First Home Owner Grant (currently $15,000 for new homes) and stamp duty concessions for first home buyers. Eligibility depends on the property value and your circumstances.

What happens on settlement day?

Settlement is when ownership of the property officially transfers. In practice, it's mostly a behind-the-scenes process. Funds are transferred, documents are lodged with Land Services SA, and the title is updated.

If you're buying, settlement day is when you get the keys. If you're selling, it's when you get paid. We coordinate with the bank, the other party's conveyancer, and Land Services SA. We'll let you know as soon as it's done.

Most settlements in SA now happen electronically through PEXA, which means they're faster and more reliable than the old paper-based process.

Can you help if I'm buying and selling at the same time?

Absolutely. This is common, and the main thing to get right is the timing. You want your sale to settle before (or at the same time as) your purchase so the funds are available. We'll coordinate both transactions and keep an eye on the settlement dates to make sure everything lines up.

If you're in this situation, get in touch early. The more notice we have, the easier it is to manage the timing.

Do I need a conveyancer for a family transfer?

Yes. Even though no money might be changing hands, the legal requirements are essentially the same as a regular property sale. The transfer documents need to be prepared correctly, stamp duty needs to be assessed (there may be exemptions depending on your circumstances), and the title needs to be updated with Land Services SA.

We handle family transfers regularly, including separations, adding or removing names, inheritance arrangements, and estate planning. More about family transfers
