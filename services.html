Our Services

Every property transaction is different. Here's how we look after yours.

Every property transaction involves contracts, searches, compliance, and coordination with multiple parties. That's what we do, and we'll keep you informed every step of the way.

Service

Property Purchases & Sales

Whether you're buying your first home, upgrading, downsizing, or selling an investment property, we look after the conveyancing from the moment contracts are signed through to settlement day.

What that actually involves: We review the contract of sale and explain everything in it. We conduct title searches to make sure there are no surprises with the property. We coordinate with the other party's conveyancer, your bank or broker, and any other parties involved. We prepare all the documentation required for settlement and keep everything on schedule.

For first home buyers: If this is your first property purchase, we know it can feel overwhelming. There's a lot of new terminology and a lot of paperwork. We'll walk you through each step so you understand what's happening and why. You can call us whenever you've got a question. There's no such thing as a silly one.

For investors and experienced buyers: If you've bought before, you already know the process. We'll keep things efficient, flag anything unusual in the contract, and keep settlement on track.

Land Divisions

Dividing land is one of the more complex property transactions, whether you're subdividing a block, adjusting a boundary, or combining titles. There are council approvals, survey requirements, and compliance steps that all need to line up.

What we take care of: We manage the conveyancing side of the land division process. That includes preparing and lodging the necessary documentation with Land Services SA, coordinating with your surveyor and council, and seeing it through until the new titles are issued.

Who this is for: Property owners looking to subdivide, developers creating new allotments, or anyone adjusting boundaries. If you're not sure whether what you're planning counts as a land division, get in touch and we'll help you figure it out.

Family Transfers

Transferring property between family members is more common than most people realise. It might be part of a separation, an inheritance arrangement, adding or removing a name from a title, or restructuring for estate planning purposes.

What's involved: We prepare the transfer documents, manage stamp duty considerations, coordinate with Land Services SA, and see the title through to completion. If there are existing mortgages on the property, we'll work with the bank to sort that out too.

Why you need a conveyancer for this: Even though no money might be changing hands, the legal requirements are the same as a regular property transaction. The transfer needs to be done properly. Wrong paperwork or missed steps can create problems down the track, especially with stamp duty or future sales.

These transactions can be sensitive. We approach them with care and discretion, and we'll explain the process clearly so everyone involved knows what to expect.

Form 1 Documents

If you're selling a property in South Australia, you're legally required to provide the buyer with a Form 1, also known as a Vendor's Statement. It's a document that discloses important information about the property, including title details, encumbrances, council rates, and any other matters the buyer needs to know about.

Why it matters: If the Form 1 is incomplete or inaccurate, the buyer has the right to void the contract. That can mean a sale falling through weeks into the process, and nobody wants that.

What we do: We prepare your Form 1 by gathering all the required information: title searches, council and water rate certificates, planning information, and any other relevant details. We review everything to make sure it's accurate and compliant before it goes to the buyer.

If you're selling through an agent, they'll usually ask you to have the Form 1 ready before listing. We can turn these around quickly. Just get in touch with your property details and we'll take it from there.

Private Contracts

Not every property transaction goes through an agent. Sometimes it's a private sale between neighbours, a deal agreed at auction without an agent, or a custom arrangement that doesn't fit the standard process.

What we do: We draft the contract of sale, making sure all the terms, conditions, and legal requirements are covered. We'll work with both parties (or their representatives) to get the contract right: fair, accurate, and protective of your interests.

Why this matters: A poorly drafted contract can lead to disputes, delays, or financial loss. Even if you've agreed on everything verbally, the written contract needs to reflect that properly, and it needs to meet the legal requirements for property transactions in South Australia.

If you've got a transaction that doesn't quite fit the usual mould, give us a call. We're happy to talk through how we can help.
